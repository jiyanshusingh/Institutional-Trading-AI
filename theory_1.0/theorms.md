Theorem 1 — Swing Uniqueness Principle
Statement

A Swing Candidate shall correspond to a unique local extremum within the observation window defined by the declared Theory.

If multiple observations share the maximum high (or minimum low) within the observation window, no Swing Candidate is established.

Rationale

A Swing represents a structurally significant turning point.

A turning point must be uniquely identifiable.

If multiple observations equally satisfy the extremum condition, the Observation History does not determine a unique turning point.

Therefore, no Swing Candidate can be established.

Consequences

This theorem implies:

Equal highs do not establish a Swing High Candidate.
Equal lows do not establish a Swing Low Candidate.
A Swing Candidate always corresponds to exactly one observation.
Every confirmed Swing is derived from a unique candidate.
Computational Implication

Any implementation conforming to Theory 1.0 shall reject observation windows in which the local maximum or local minimum is not unique.

This is an implementation consequence of the theorem, not the theorem itself.
