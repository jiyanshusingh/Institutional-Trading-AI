# 07_OBSERVATION_HISTORY.md

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)
**Status:** Normative Object Specification

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of the Observation History is to represent the complete, immutable set of market observations over which a declared theory constructs a semantic interpretation.

Observation History is the unique input aggregate to the Semantic Construction Pipeline.

It establishes the scope of interpretation and defines the observational boundary of the Canonical Market Model.

---

# 2. Definition

An **Observation History** is the immutable value object representing a finite sequence of market observations together with the contextual metadata required to interpret those observations under a declared theory.

It represents **what was observed**, not **what those observations mean**.

No semantic interpretation is contained within the Observation History.

---

# 3. Scope

An Observation History always represents exactly one observation sequence.

Its scope is determined by:

* one financial instrument;
* one timeframe;
* one observation interval;
* one declared observation domain.

It shall never combine observations belonging to different instruments or incompatible observation domains.

---

# 4. Identity

Observation History has **no identity**.

It is a Value Object.

Two Observation Histories are equal if they represent the same observation sequence together with identical contextual metadata.

Identity is determined entirely by semantic value rather than object identity.

---

# 5. Source of Truth

The Source of Truth of an Observation History is the immutable Observation Space.

The Observation History introduces no interpretation.

It preserves the observations exactly as they exist within the declared observation domain.

---

# 6. Containment

An Observation History aggregates observational information only.

It may contain:

* observation sequence;
* observation metadata;
* observation boundaries.

It shall not contain:

* semantic constructs;
* evaluations;
* decisions;
* representations;
* predictions;
* execution information.

Observation History defines the observational context from which semantic interpretation is derived.

---

# 7. Responsibilities

The Observation History is responsible for:

* representing the complete observation sequence;
* preserving observational integrity;
* defining the scope of interpretation;
* providing deterministic input to semantic construction.

It is **not** responsible for:

* semantic interpretation;
* validation of market theories;
* evaluation;
* decision-making;
* execution;
* storage implementation.

---

# 8. Lifetime

Observation History is immutable.

Once established, its observations and metadata shall never change.

Any modification results in a new Observation History.

---

# 9. Equality

Observation History is compared by value.

Two Observation Histories are equal if they contain:

* identical observations;
* identical observation ordering;
* identical observation metadata;
* identical observation boundaries.

Implementation identity shall not participate in equality.

---

# 10. Invariants

Every valid Observation History shall satisfy the following invariants.

## Invariant 1 — Immutable

Observations shall never change after establishment.

---

## Invariant 2 — Ordered

Observations shall preserve their canonical chronological ordering.

---

## Invariant 3 — Complete

The observation sequence shall completely represent the declared observation interval.

---

## Invariant 4 — Consistent

Every observation shall conform to the declared observation schema.

---

## Invariant 5 — Homogeneous

All observations shall belong to the same observation domain.

Examples include:

* identical symbol;
* identical timeframe;
* identical observation format.

---

## Invariant 6 — Deterministic

Identical observation sources shall produce identical Observation Histories.

---

## Invariant 7 — Self-Contained

All information required for semantic interpretation shall be available within the Observation History or its declared metadata.

No downstream layer shall require external observational context.

---

# 11. Construction

Observation History shall be established exactly once from an Observation Space.

Conceptually:

```text
Observation Space
        │
        ▼
Observation History
```

Construction shall preserve observational integrity.

No semantic interpretation occurs during construction.

---

# 12. Public Contract

The Observation History guarantees:

* a complete observation sequence;
* deterministic ordering;
* immutable observations;
* consistent observational metadata;
* one observation scope;
* one observation interval.

Consumers may rely on these guarantees without inspecting the construction process.

---

# 13. Relationship to the Semantic Construction Pipeline

The Observation History is the unique input to the Semantic Construction Pipeline.

Conceptually:

```text
ObservationHistory
        │
        ▼
Declared Theory
        │
        ▼
SemanticConstructionPipeline
        │
        ▼
CanonicalMarketModel
```

The pipeline interprets the Observation History according to the declared Theory.

It does not modify the Observation History.

---

# 14. Relationship to the Canonical Market Model

The Observation History and the Canonical Market Model have complementary responsibilities.

The Observation History represents:

> **What was observed.**

The Canonical Market Model represents:

> **What those observations mean under a declared Theory.**

Neither object subsumes the other.

The Canonical Market Model is always interpreted with respect to exactly one Observation History.

---

# 15. Versioning

Every Observation History shall record sufficient metadata to ensure reproducibility.

This may include:

* observation source;
* observation interval;
* symbol;
* timeframe;
* observation schema version.

Versioning supports deterministic reconstruction but does not affect semantic interpretation.

---

# 16. Excluded Concepts

The following concepts do **not** belong to Observation History:

* Swing
* Structure Event
* Expansion
* Origin Region
* Fair Value Gap
* Order Block
* Evaluation
* Trade Setup
* Machine Learning Features
* Market State
* Trading Signals

These are products of downstream interpretation rather than observation.

---

# 17. Conclusion

Observation History is the immutable input aggregate of the Institutional Trading AI framework.

It provides the complete observational context required for semantic interpretation while remaining independent of theory, ontology, computation, evaluation, and implementation.

Together with a declared Theory, it forms the complete input to the Semantic Construction Pipeline:

```text
(ObservationHistory, Theory)
            │
            ▼
SemanticConstructionPipeline
            │
            ▼
CanonicalMarketModel
```

By separating observation from interpretation, the framework preserves semantic clarity, deterministic behavior, reproducibility, and architectural separation of concerns.
